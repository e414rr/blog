A narrator may be close to, or even part of, the action in a work of fiction, or distant in time and/or space. What is the effect of either the closeness or the distance of narrators?

The narrator telling a story can make a story sound how they want to make it sound. They set the tone for the whole book. Their choice of language is impactful and can foreshadow events in a fiction book. Especially in works from the Gothic and Romantic era. Narrators can also be fully or limited omniscient and tell the reader everything a chracter is feeling. The drawback comes in when talking about other characters in the book. If the narrator is speaking in a first person point of view then we only know what that character is feeling and we don't know what other characters are feeling or their emotions.
